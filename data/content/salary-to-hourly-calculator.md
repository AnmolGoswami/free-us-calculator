## How the Salary to Hourly Calculator Works

The basic formula we use is straightforward and accurate:

**Hourly Rate = Annual Salary ÷ (Hours per Week × Weeks per Year)**

We also calculate:
- **Daily Rate** = Hourly Rate × Hours per Day
- **Weekly Rate** = Hourly Rate × Hours per Week
- **Monthly Equivalent** = Annual Salary ÷ 12

You can adjust the number of weeks per year to account for vacation time, holidays, or sick days (most people use 50–52 weeks).

For non-exempt hourly workers, the calculator also helps estimate potential overtime pay at 1.5x the regular rate for hours over 40 per week.

## Pro Tips for Using Salary to Hourly in the USA

- Always factor in **actual hours worked**, not just scheduled hours — many salaried roles expect 45–55+ hours
- Consider **cost of living** — $90,000 in Texas goes much further than $120,000 in San Francisco or New York
- For freelancers: Add 25–40% to your target hourly rate to cover self-employment taxes, health insurance, and no paid time off
- Non-exempt employees: Know your rights — overtime over 40 hours must usually be paid at 1.5x your regular hourly rate
- Use net (after-tax) hourly rate when comparing to gig economy work like DoorDash, Uber, or consulting
- Re-calculate after every raise, bonus, or change in work schedule

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### How do I convert annual salary to hourly rate?
Divide your annual salary by the total number of hours you work in a year (Hours per week × 52). Example: $100,000 ÷ (40 × 52) = $48.08 per hour.

### Should I use 40 hours or more?
Use 40 hours for standard calculations. If you regularly work more, run the numbers at 45 or 50 hours to see your true effective rate.

### Does this calculator include taxes?
The main results show gross hourly rate. You can combine it with our USA Paycheck Calculator for estimated net (take-home) hourly pay.

### Can freelancers and contractors use this tool?
Yes! Many independent contractors use it to set competitive hourly billing rates that cover taxes and business expenses.

### What is a good hourly rate in the USA in 2026?
It varies widely by industry, location, and experience. Tech roles often range from $50–$120+, healthcare $35–$80, and general office jobs $20–$45 per hour.

### How does unpaid overtime affect my hourly rate?
It lowers your effective hourly rate significantly. Always calculate based on actual hours worked.
