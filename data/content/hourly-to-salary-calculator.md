## Salary Structure & Tax Information in India 2026 (What You Should Know)

**Typical CTC Breakdown for a ₹12 Lakh Package:**
- Basic Salary: 40–50% of CTC
- House Rent Allowance (HRA): 15–40% (gives tax benefits if you pay rent)
- Special Allowance / Other Allowances: Remaining flexible part
- Employer’s PF Contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Gratuity and other benefits

**Common Deductions from Your Gross Salary:**
- Employee PF Contribution: 12%
- Professional Tax: ₹200–₹300 per month (varies by state)
- TDS (Income Tax): Depends on your tax regime and slabs
- ESI Contribution (if your salary is below the threshold)

In 2026, the **New Tax Regime** is still popular because of lower rates, but it has fewer deductions. For quick estimates, use 10–30% effective tax rate depending on your income level.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Salary in India 2026

### What is ₹50,000 monthly salary in hourly rate?
At 48 hours/week: ≈ ₹240 per hour  
At 40 hours/week: ≈ ₹288 per hour

### How do I convert annual CTC to hourly rate?
Just divide Annual CTC by (Hours per week × 52).  
Example: ₹15 Lakh ÷ (45 hours × 52) ≈ ₹641 per hour.

### Does this calculator include overtime pay?
Yes! There’s a dedicated overtime mode where you can add extra hours and choose 1.5x or 2x multiplier.

### Is overtime always paid double in India?
For hourly and contract workers, yes — usually 2x the basic rate as per labour laws. For salaried employees, it depends on company policy (sometimes compensatory off instead).

### How accurate is the tax estimate?
It uses a flat effective rate for quick calculations. For exact figures, use a detailed Income Tax calculator with your exact deductions.

### Can freelancers and consultants use this tool?
Absolutely. Many freelancers use it to decide their hourly rates or compare freelance income with full-time job offers.

### What is the best hours per week to use?
40 hours is the standard assumption, but 45–48 hours is more realistic for most corporate and private sector jobs in India.
