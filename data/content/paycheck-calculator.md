## What is a USA Paycheck Calculator?

A paycheck calculator (also called a take-home pay calculator or net pay calculator) shows you exactly how much of your gross salary you’ll actually keep after all taxes and deductions are removed.

In the United States, especially in high-tax states like California, the difference between your gross salary and net paycheck can be surprisingly large. Many people are shocked when they see their first paycheck — that’s why using an accurate 2026 paycheck calculator is so important.

Our tool includes:
- Federal income tax (2026 brackets)
- California state income tax
- FICA taxes (Social Security + Medicare)
- Standard deductions and common pre-tax benefits

Just enter your annual salary, pay frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, semi-monthly, or monthly), filing status, and number of dependents, and you’ll get an instant, realistic estimate.



## Taxes Included in the 2026 USA Paycheck Calculator

Here’s exactly what this calculator factors in for California residents:

### 1. Federal Income Tax
Uses the latest 2026 federal tax brackets and standard deduction. Your effective federal tax rate depends on your total income and filing status (Single, Married Filing Jointly, Head of Household, etc.).

### 2. California State Income Tax
California has one of the highest state income tax rates in the country, with brackets going up to 13.3% for high earners. This calculator uses the current 2026 California tax tables so you get an accurate picture.

### 3. FICA Taxes (Social Security + Medicare)
- Social Security: 6.2% on earnings up to the annual wage base (projected around $176,100 in 2026)
- Medicare: 1.45% (plus Additional Medicare Tax of 0.9% for high earners)

These are automatically deducted from every paycheck.

### 4. Other Common Deductions
- Health insurance premiums (if pre-tax)
- 401(k) or retirement contributions
- HSA/FSA contributions
- State disability insurance (SDI) in California
