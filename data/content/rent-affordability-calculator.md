# Rent Affordability Calculator – How Much Rent Can I Afford?

Use our free rent affordability calculator to determine the maximum rent you can comfortably pay based on your income in 2026.

## The 30% Rule Explained

Financial experts recommend spending no more than **30%** of your gross monthly income on rent. This calculator follows the 30% rule while also considering your existing debts.

## Why Rent Affordability Matters

- Prevents financial stress
- Helps avoid becoming "rent burdened"
- Better budgeting for other expenses like food, transport, and savings
- Useful when moving to expensive cities like New York, San Francisco, or Los Angeles

## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter your monthly take-home income
2. Add any existing monthly debts (car loan, student loan, credit card, etc.)
3. See recommended rent based on conservative 30-35% rules

## Pro Tips for Renting in 2026

- Always budget for utilities, internet, and parking separately
- Consider transportation costs if the apartment is far from work
- Factor in annual rent increases (typically 3-5%)
- Maintain an emergency fund of at least 3-6 months of expenses
